Winston Churchill

The man who never gave up, and inspired a nation to do the same

Here is a timeline of Winston Churchill's life:

  • 1874 - Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill is born on November 30th in Blenheim Palace, Oxfordshire, England.
  • 1895 - Commissioned as a second lieutenant in the British Army.
  • 1899-1902 - Serves as a war correspondent during the Second Boer War in South Africa.
  • 1901 - Elected to Parliament as a Conservative MP for Oldham.
  • 1904 - Defects to the Liberal Party and is appointed Under-Secretary of State for the Colonies.
  • 1911-1915 - Serves as First Lord of the Admiralty, overseeing the Royal Navy during World War I.
  • 1915-1916 - Demoted and serves as Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster.
  • 1917-1922 - Serves as Minister of Munitions and Secretary of State for War and Air.
  • 1924-1929 - Serves as Chancellor of the Exchequer.
  • 1939 - Appointed First Lord of the Admiralty again as World War II begins.
  • 1940 - Becomes Prime Minister of Great Britain, leading the country through the darkest days of the war.
  • 1946 - Delivers his famous "Iron Curtain" speech in Fulton, Missouri, warning of Soviet expansionism.
  • 1951 - Becomes Prime Minister again, serving until 1955.
  • 1953 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for his writings and speeches.
  • January 24, 1965 - Dies at the age of 90.
"Churchill was the greatest Englishman of his time, and one of the greatest of all time. He was a master of words, a genius of leadership, and a friend to freedom."
- Ronald Reagan, former President of the United States
